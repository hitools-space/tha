What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is an act of copying another person's work or ideas and presenting them as your own without giving credit. It can happen in writing, speeches, artwork, or even code. Whether you plagiarize intentionally or accidentally, this practice is considered dishonest and can have serious consequences. That’s why it’s important to check your content for plagiarism before publishing or submitting it.

Types of Plagiarism

The common types of plagiarism you can identify with the help of our plagiarism checker are discussed below. 

  • Direct Plagiarism

Direct plagiarism is defined as the word-by-word copying of a text without citing the source, providing any credits, or adding quotation marks. 

  • Self-Plagiarism

Reusing words from your previously written and published articles is regarded as self-plagiarism. Although self-plagiarism doesn’t involve stealing others’ ideas, it still has similar consequences as other types of plagiarism.

  • Patchwork Plagiarism

Patchwork plagiarism is also known as mosaic plagiarism. If you copy passages, ideas, or phrases from different sentences and present them all as new text, such duplication is known as patchwork plagiarism. 

  • Accidental Plagiarism

When someone doesn’t intend to copy someone’s ideas or words and unintentionally writes the same phrases, words, or sentences in their text, it is called accidental plagiarism.
