Different Citation Styles

Citation style is an important element that a student needs to be aware of in order to cite sources in their assignments adequately. There are mainly three types of citation styles: APA, MLA, and Chicago. The formatting and styling of each of these formats differ from one another.

APA

For citing sources in APA, you need the name of the author, year of publication, title of the source, and publisher's name. For the in-text citation of APA format, you need to mention the second name and year of publication at the end of the information you’re citing in brackets.

MLA

MLA citation requires students to follow specific guidelines, which involve in-text citations, works cited pages, abbreviations, and quotations. While forming a works cited list in MLA style, you need to write down the author’s name, title, publisher, and year of publication for each source.

Chicago

Chicago citation follows two types of citation styles, which include author-date and notes and bibliography. In the author-date style, you need to cite sources briefly in the text and provide their match-up in the references list.
