What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is the value derived from the height and weight of men and women. It is a widely used metric for categorizing a person as obese, overweight, average, or underweight. BMI is calculated in the units of kilogram per meter square, as it is measured by dividing the square of a body's height by a body mass. 

BMI Categories:

Generally, you can consider yourself one of them if your BMI falls between the given ranges:

  • Obese BMI = If it's equal to or greater than 30
  • Overweight = BMI is equal to or between 25 - 29.9
  • Normal Weight = BMI is equal to or between 18.5 - 24.9
  • Underweight BMI= Less than 18.5

Risks Associated with Being Overweight

Being overweight leads a person to several health issues. You can face the following risks with being overweight:

  • High blood pressure
  • High LDL cholesterol
  • Heart disease
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Breathing issues
  • Mental disorders
  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Body pain

Risks Associated with Being Underweight

Being underweight can also lead to medical problems, just like obesity. The significant risks you can face being underweight are:

  • Sickness
  • Anemia
  • Feeling tired
  • Slow growth
  • Premature birth
  • Irregular periods
  • Skin and hair problems 

How Do I Calculate BMI Manually?

There are two main formulas to calculate BMI, which are English BMI and Metric BMI. Let’s take a look at each of them and understand how they work with the help of an example. 

English BMI Formula:

= 703 x weight (lbs.)/ height2 (inches)

With this BMI formula, you need to multiply the weight in lbs. by 703 and divide it by the height squared in inches. For instance, if a person’s weight is 160 lbs. and height is 70 inches, then his BMI would be calculated as:

= 703 x 160/ 702

BMI = 22.9

Metric BMI Formula:

= Weight (Kgs)/ height2 (meters)

The metric BMI formula is similar to the English formula, but the only difference between them is the measurement units. If a person’s height is 1.8 meters and weight is 100 kg, then with the metric formula, his BMI would be calculated as:

= 100/1.82

BMI = 30.9

Limitations of BMI

Experts have raised their fingers over the reliability of the Body Mass Index due to several reasons. There are certain limitations of BMI due to which they have termed it an inaccurate measure for analyzing body fat. Some of its limitations are:

  • BMI charts don't distinguish between the gender of the individuals. Males are prone to gaining muscle mass, whereas females have fat mass, which makes BMI an unrealistic measure.
  • BMI doesn’t take body fat percentage or muscle mass into account.
  • BMI doesn’t specify water weight and bone mass while calculating the relation between height and weight.

Who Shouldn’t Use a BMI Calculator?

BMI wouldn’t be an accurate representation for many people, such as pregnant women, athletes, young children, and bodybuilders. This is because a BMI calculator considers weight just as a number; however, for some people, the weight could be heavy due to muscles rather than fats.

For instance, athletes and bodybuilders have a high muscle mass that results in a much higher BMI. But this doesn’t mean they are open to facing health issues like obese people. Similarly, during pregnancy, the mass and body of a woman change, which makes the BMI calculator ineffective for them. Moreover, BMI calculation will show low BMI for children as they are likely to lose mass. But they shouldn’t be considered underweight as they are still at a growth stage.

FAQs 

  • When Should I Be Concerned with My Child's Weight?

If your child is losing weight, getting weak, staying ill for ample time, or has a lasting illness, you should be concerned and talk to your doctor.

  • When Should I Start Calculating BMI? How Often Should I Check?

You can start BMI calculation whenever you want. For children, it must be checked for the ones who are 2 years old. You can calculate your BMI once every four weeks.

  • What is a Healthy BMI?

The healthy BMI ranges from 18.5 to 24.9. Moreover, underweight BMI is less than 18.5, and overweight BMI ranges from 25.0 to 29.9.

  • What is My Ideal Weight?

Your ideal body weight depends upon your sex, age, and height. There’s no specific figure of ideal weight for all individuals.

  • Is Muscle Counted in BMI?

No! The muscle mass is not taken into account for calculating BMI

  • How Can I Reduce My BMI?

You can take several actions to reduce your BMI. You should ditch highly processed foods, increase fiber intake, and make use of protein, to reduce your BMI.

  • How Can I Help my Child Reduce his/her BMI?

You can help your child in reducing BMI by encouraging him/her to participate in physical activities daily. You can make them follow strict diets and take healthy meals.

  • BMI Reduction: How Long Will It Take?

You can reduce between 1-3% of your body fat per month. BMI reduction depends upon how seriously you take it and how much effort you are investing.
