Check SSL Certificate Authenticity With Online SSL Test

As mentioned earlier, the SSL certificate works as a guarantee that the website is trustworthy and you can use it without worrying. It binds information like server name, company name, domain name, and location of the company together. The details of every single visitor are encrypted and not shared anywhere.

Anyone can see whether a website has this certificate or not through the navigation bar. If a website has an SSL certificate its URL comes up with HTTPS on the start. However, just having it isn’t enough; the authenticity of that certificate is also essential. A website owner can test SSL certificate is authentic by using an SSL certificate checker.

Our SSL checker online is a free and handy online tool, which you can use to run an SSL server test. An SSL test is a way to see if it's correctly installed, valid, credible, and not creating any difficulty for your visitors.

Before running an SSL test, you must get one, and that isn’t much trouble these days. There are different companies that sell these certificates. SSL certificates can be found easily with different prices and can be renewed annually. The process will require you to give details about your website, address, contact, and owners, etc. These details are part of your public key in the SSL certificate.

Impact Of SSL Certificate On Search Ranking

In 2014, Google declared that all the websites having an SSL certificate might enjoy a little improvement in their search engine ranking.

Search engines, especially Google, values SSL security.

The fact that you are here indicates perhaps you are already familiar with SSL certification. In simple words, this certificate gives particular encryption to a site, which secures any information by only keeping it between users and websites. It also makes sure that any third parties, including hackers and other online thieves, are not able to read the data. That is particularly critical during online shopping when you have to enter payment details to proceed. Thus, it gives protection to the information.

Getting this certificate has become a standard, and a must-do for websites to secure the trust of search engines and visitors alike. Google also informed the public that they would highlight the websites, which are not secure.

These measures are implemented to not only inform the internet users that their data might not be secured with websites without an SSL certificate. But, it also forces the owners of the site to purchase the certificate.

Did you know: You can check whether any webpage is secured or not by merely looking at the navigation bar on the search bar. If the bar has a padlock icon, then the site is said to be secure.

Why Is It Worth To Test SSL Certificate

There are many reasons for which the website owners need to have an SSL certificate, some of which we have already discussed. Some of the key reasons are:

Approves your identity:

SSL certificate gives verification to any site. This authentication plays a significant role when it comes to online security. Website verification is the same as verified accounts on social media accounts. However, the SSL certificate does not allow any other website to make a fake version of yours. This will enable users to differentiate between the real and counterfeit websites; helping them steer clear of fraudulent sites.

Builds User Trust:

Other than just authentication, an SSL certificate is also crucial for gaining trust in the eyes of users, especially if you are running an e-commerce website. The ‘lock’ icon in the navigation bar is game changes, which can make or break the game for you. It signals site visitors that their data will remain secure. Hence, they can visit or make transactions through your website without any fear.

Became a Google Standard:

In 2018, for a better and secured online experience, Google announced to flag the website that does not have TLS or SSL certificates. You should be aware that the address bar of web browsers will show a “not secure” sign if your site doesn’t have an SSL certificate. Not to mention, how that can adversely affect not only reputation but also the search engine ranking of your website.
