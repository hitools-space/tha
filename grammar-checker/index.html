Common Grammatical Errors Our Free Grammar Checker Detects

This free grammar checker conducts an in-depth grammar check on your uploaded text to help you make as many sentence corrections as possible. You can use this tool to check grammar mistakes without anyone’s assistance. The types of grammatical errors our tool can detect include the following.

Subject-Verb Agreement

This grammar rule is one of the most necessary to follow in writing. It involves the usage of the correct verbs as per the subject in the sentences. This free grammar checker can highlight such errors from your text and help you to make grammatical error corrections.

Grammar Error: Zack like fast food.
Correct Grammar: Zack likes fast food.

Active/Passive Voice Problem

Active voice is considered better than passive voice because it is less complicated than passive voice. This  grammar checker smartly detects such issues from any kind of text and provides recommendations to fix them.

Passive: The thieves were being chased by the cops. 
Active: The cops were chasing the thieves.

Article Usage (Definite & Indefinite)

The articles have a significant role in English grammar, ensuring that your sentences are grammatically correct. It's important to understand when to use definite and indefinite articles. Many people get confused about whether to use "a" or "an"? "The" or none at all. If you're unsure about your grammar, you can use our free grammar checker to help identify and correct these issues in your writing.

Grammar Error: I’m going to park.
Correct Grammar: I’m going to the park.

Incorrect Apostrophes

Apostrophes have different uses in the English language, and people often get confused while using them.  It's common to mix up "its" and "it's," along with other similar words.  If you have difficulty using apostrophes properly in your writing, there's no need to worry. This grammar checker can help you make the necessary corrections easily and without complicated procedures.

Grammar Error: What’s your's will find you.
Correct Grammar: What’s yours will find you.

Misspelled Words

Misspellings can occur even when you are writing carefully. The grammar checker identifies them immediately and provides the correct spelling without interrupting your writing flow. With a single click, you can correct all the misspelled words.

Confusing or Redundant Words

When you write in a natural flow, your words can sometimes become confusing or repetitive. To avoid this issue, you can use our grammar checker online. It gently highlights any redundancy and suggests clearer alternatives. In short, it helps you eliminate excess wording and sharpen your message.

What is a Run-on Sentence Error in English Writing?

The writing errors caused by the use of inappropriate punctuation are known as run-on sentence errors. For example, if you insert a comma at the end of a sentence instead of a full stop, and start writing the following sentence, it will cause a run-on sentence error.
